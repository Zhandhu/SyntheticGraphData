This is DISTINCT from uncertainty injection (which only reframes the same question).
You are adding genuine new knowledge requirements by pulling in adjacent graph entities.

ESCALATION STRATEGIES — choose ONE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. HOP_EXTENSION
   Add one more reasoning hop. Take a key entity from the current question and ask
   about a property of an entity related to it — one step further in the graph.
   Example: "What is X's Y?" → "What is the Z of the entity whose Y matches X's Y?"

2. SUBGRAPH_BRIDGING
   Connect two currently separate clusters. Extend the question so it requires facts
   from a distant part of the graph, connected to the current subgraph through an
   intermediate relationship. The solver must traverse across graph clusters.

3. MULTI_ENTITY_AGGREGATION
   Require aggregating or comparing across multiple graph entities. Transform a
   single-entity lookup into a question that examines all relevant candidates and
   synthesizes their properties — ranking, finding max/min, computing totals, or
   identifying the unique one satisfying all conditions.

4. CONSTRAINT_CHAIN
   Add a chain of filtering constraints, each sourced from a different entity or
   relation in the graph. The solver must apply each constraint in sequence to narrow
   the answer space, with no single entity providing all the constraints.

5. INVERSE_REASONING
   Flip the reasoning direction. Instead of a forward lookup (given X, find property Y),
   require inverse inference (given a property value Y, find which entity X has it,
   subject to additional graph-derived conditions). Forces search rather than retrieval.
